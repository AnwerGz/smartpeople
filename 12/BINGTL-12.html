Excessive gadget use among kids is dangerous. We should limit the time of gadget use and accompany them when they use it. Nowadays, they can easily access gadgets. Their parents give them gadgets to increase their knowledge, to make them stay at home, or to make them calm. This has an increasingly concerning negative impact on their development. A rise in speech, attention, and learning issues defines this.

To begin with, kids who use gadgets too often more easily find contents that have not been filtered with their age. Unfiltered contents can lead to psychological disorders in kids such as a decrease in kids' level of self-control. For example, contents of animal abuse, hate speech, or violence, can influence their mind and behavior.

Kids also tend to be emotionally affected when not given gadgets and are unable to control themselves when using gadgets. They cry and make noises until they are given gadgets and when the gadgets are taken from them, they cry again and become tantrum.

Kids also tend to have less social interaction which can cause difficulties and fears when communicating with the surrounding environment for using gadgets too long. They do not care about the surrounding as they focus on the contents in their gadgets. This makes them difficult to interact with others. They feel awkward when they have to interact with others because they often become passive viewers of contents without having the chance to express their thought.

In addition, fear and depression were also found in kids. This can happen if they have a strong bond with the figures in the contents or when they are verbally abused by the net users.

The negative impacts above raise concern. Therefore, the use of gadget among kids must be limited to improve their speech, cognitive, and social skills.

In the rapidly evolving world of education, deep learning has become an essential approach to fostering critical thinking and problem-solving skills. Unlike rote memorization, deep learning encourages students to engage in meaningful understanding, making connections between concepts and applying knowledge in real-world scenarios. 

This approach is crucial in preparing students for the complexities of the modern workforce and society, emphasizing the need for its integration into educational systems. One key reason deep learning is vital in education is its ability to enhance students' cognitive engagement. When students actively analyze, evaluate, and create knowledge rather than merely recalling information, they develop a more profound understanding of subjects. This process allows them to retain information for longer periods and apply it flexibly across various disciplines.

Furthermore, deep learning fosters creativity and innovation, enabling students to approach problems with critical thinking and novel solutions. Another significant benefit of deep learning is its role in equipping students with 21st-century skills. The modern job market demands individuals who can adapt to new technologies, think critically, and collaborate effectively. Traditional learning methods often fall short in preparing students for such challenges. By incorporating deep learning techniques, such as project-based learning and inquiry-driven education, students develop adaptability, teamwork, and decision-making skills that are indispensable in today’s professional landscape. 

In conclusion, deep learning is a crucial component of modern education as it promotes cognitive engagement and prepares students for future challenges. By emphasizing critical thinking, creativity, and adaptability, deep learning ensures that students are not just passive recipients of knowledge but active participants in their own learning journey. Therefore, integrating deep learning strategies into education systems should be a priority to equip students with the necessary skills to thrive in an ever-changing world.

Introducing free nutritious food in Indonesia has sparked a debate among experts, policymakers, and the public. However, proponents argue that providing free nutritious food can significantly improve public health, particularly among low-income families and children. Malnutrition and food insecurity are prevalent issues in Indonesia, especially in rural areas. By offering free nutritious food, the government can ensure that vulnerable populations receive the essential nutrients needed for their growth and development. This initiative can also reduce healthcare costs in the long run, as a healthier population is less likely to suffer from diet-related diseases such as obesity, diabetes, and heart disease. Furthermore, providing free nutritious food can enhance students' academic performance by improving their concentration and cognitive abilities, leading to better educational outcomes and future opportunities.

However, critics argue that implementing a free nutritious food program poses significant challenges and potential drawbacks. The funding cost program could strain the government's budget, potentially leading to cuts in other essential services. Additionally, there is a risk of mismanagement and corruption in the distribution process, which could result in the food not reaching those who need it most. Some critics also believe that providing free food might create a culture of dependency, discouraging individuals from seeking employment or improving their economic situation. Instead of offering free food, they argue that the government should focus on creating job opportunities and improving agricultural productivity to ensure food security and self-sufficiency in the long term.

In conclusion, while free nutritious food can provide immediate relief to those in need and promote public health, it is essential to carefully consider the potential challenges and long-term implications of such a program. A balanced approach that addresses both immediate needs and sustainable solutions may be the most effective way to improve food security and overall well-being in Indonesia.
